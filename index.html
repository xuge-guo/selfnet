DOT: Lifelike Quadruped Locomotion

Designed, fabricated, and programmed from scratch. Built a MuJoCo simulator and trained a PPO policy (>50M steps) to explore gait ideas in simulation. For the physical robot, I hand‑designed and tuned the deployed gait, achieving a stable walking speed of 0.40 m/s.

Roles: mechanical design, controls, ML · Tools: MuJoCo, SB3, Python, Raspberry Pi

Other Project

Two‑Wheel Target‑Tracking Robot — Best Use of Computer Vision (MakeCU Hackathon)

Nov 2025

Built in 24 hours at MakeCU, this Raspberry Pi–based two‑wheel robot performs autonomous target tracking and obstacle avoidance. A webcam and ultrasonic rangefinder localize the target in real time, steer toward it, and brake when an obstacle is within 50 cm.

  • Real‑time vision for left/right target localization & differential steering
  • Ultrasonic sensing for proactive collision avoidance and auto‑stop
  • Integrated camera processing, motor control, and sensor fusion
Role: team lead · Tools: Python, OpenCV, Raspberry Pi

About

I am a robotics student focused on lifelike locomotion. I use machine learning in simulation to generate and evaluate gait ideas; for the hardware, I currently deploy hand‑designed gaits for reliability and safety. My near‑term goal is to bridge the gap with sim‑to‑real adaptation and self‑modeling so that learned policies can transfer to the robot.
